The AR Data Experience Point at HDIL is a research project on embedded temporal data visualizations in an urban environment for the exploration of traffic data in everyday life. The interaction with urban data is realized through Argumented Reality. This allows the data to be better understood embedded in the reality it represents. As can be seen in the video, a grouped area diagram visualizing the development of traffic. The time data is positioned along the axis, with arrows and legends floating slightly overhead. For the project I developed different visualization languages ranging from continuous grouped area charts, discrete stacked bar charts, grouped bar charts, violin plots, flat area charts to layered area charts for multiple time axes. The focus in the design of the visualization was on how the visualization of the data is connected to the environment.
