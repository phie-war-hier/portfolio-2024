The overheard catastrophe - the installation consists of several wooden beams set up vertically. The height of the beams varies as they represent the waveform of a sonar signal. To make the installation as sustainable as possible, it could be made from driftwood or old beams and processed to the appropriate sizes. Viewers can walk around the installation and explore the different heights of the beams while learning about the topic of underwater noise. The beams are illuminated differently by the movement of the sunlight, emphasizing the diversity of the beams and the movement of the waves. The installation is intended to raise awareness of the issue of underwater noise.
