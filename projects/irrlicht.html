Sophie Humbert

Can data be presented in a way that both draws attention to a particular issue and inspires with its aesthetic form? Irrlicht is an immersive installation that aims to create a deeper awareness of the issue of light pollution so that it critically reflects current circumstances. To this end, the installation uses the method of data materialization, which gives viewers an emotional and subjective understanding of the data.

Light pollution occurs when artificial light enters the atmosphere and is reflected by dust and water. Scientists have found that the brightness of the night sky is increasing exponentially every year, meaning that by 2030 the Earth could be twice as bright as it is today. Irrlicht uses a person's position to adjust the light intensity depending on the real light pollution in Mannheim in a room. With each step, the viewer feels the effects on brightness and auditory elements, which become louder when the light intensity is high.

My research on the subject of light pollution, the method of data materialization and the documentation of the Irrlicht installation have been presented in the booklet “Irrlicht - Data Materialization on Light Pollution”.
